Slow Hot Computer

Launch project

Slow Hot Computer is a website that makes your computer run slow and hot. Use it at work to decrease your productivity.

The site works by perpetually downloading an image file in the background. I employed a free service called Peek to test it out on a variety of of operating systems and browsers. Here's a video of one of the tests:

Slow Hot Computer accomplishes two things: it makes the user's computer run slowly to the point of being almost unusable, but falls short of actually crashing the computer. This allows the user to continue working but severely hampers their productivity, an action that provides a venue for resistance that is less confrontational, and therefore safer, than full out strike or refusal to work. Bosses and managers call acts like these "time theft".

An additional benefit of Slow Hot Computer is that it makes the user's computer consume more energy, thus running up their employer's electricity bill.

Employers increasingly outsource their labor needs to exploited workers, to automated systems and algorithms, or to systems which are semi-automated but still rely on human labor (such as Mechanical Turk). The same tools and methodologies that lead to repressive environments can be used, in turn, for actions and gestures of resistance. We should begin to envision how to outsource and automate our resistance, how to resist algorithmically.

Slow Hot Computer is a fully functioning prototype. But more importantly it's a conceptual gesture that modernizes an old tactic. It's just one implementation of a procedure that could be realized in any number of implementations. For example, instead of running Slow Hot Computer, a user could intentionally install malware on their work computer.
