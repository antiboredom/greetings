The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change Capitalism

Launch Project

The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change, founded by the United Nations in 1988, is the "leading international body for the assessment of climate change". The organization is tasked with assessing and synthesizing (but not producing) research around climate change. Every few years it publishes a series of comprehensive reports on the current state of climate change research and knowledge.

In 2007, the Nobel Peace Prize was jointly awarded to Al Gore and the IPCC "for their efforts to build up and disseminate greater knowledge about man-made climate change, and to lay the foundations for the measures that are needed to counteract such change."

Tega Brain and I have embarked on an ambitious project to edit all material published by the IPCC: through a series of automated procedures we replace every instance of the phrase "climate change" with the word "capitalism". The Intergovernmental Panel on Capitalism is a fictional world whose foundation is a one-line joke taken to its extreme conclusion. It is also a place to ask how international environmental discourse might directly engage with the overwhelming tension between environmental agendas and economic priorities.

If you wish to learn more about the IPCC, see their Fact Sheet

We start our systematic process with the IPCC's Fifth Assessment Report (AR5): 5000 pages of text split into four subreports. We used Adobe Acrobat for the find/replace.

Afterwards we printed and bound our modified reports.

The edited reports contain a wealth of useful information and insight about capitalism.

Next, we downloaded the entire IPCC website using a tool called HTTRACK, and the did the find/replace using SED.

Not fully satisfied, I wrote a python script that does find/replace in images, using the open source optical character recognition tool tesseract.

Finally, I wrote another python script that does find/replace in video. First, the script transcribes audio and extracts timestamps for the searched word. Then, it creates a new audio track by overdubbing the found words with the desired replacement. In this case we used instances of the word "capitalism" extracted from the audiobook version of Piketty's "Capital in the Twenty-First Century." I ran the script on all 104 videos in the IPCC's YouTube channel. Many of these are videos clearly meant for internal use.

I then made a supercut of all the instances where a replacement happened.

In this project, the IPCC's organizational materials and output become a canvas onto which I can project my own fantasy. The fantasy is timid in nature: the fictional world we are building is not a utopia where private property is abolished - instead it is a light reform - all that has changed is word; the hopeless rush towards apocalyptic self-destruction remains intact. The exuberance I feel when browsing the fictional website and watching hours of fictional video quickly turns to vertigo.

The project will only truly be complete when we have made a full replica of the IPCC offices, and enlisted an international team of scientists and policy makers to study the deleterious effects of global capitalism.

In the words of the Nobel Committee: "Action is necessary now, before capitalism moves beyond man's control."
